# Full Time

The game ends one of two ways: someone reaches **rung 20** and wins instantly, or the **real match hits full time** and the highest climber takes it. Either way, you land on the full-time screen — the payoff for the whole session.

## The winner

<table><thead><tr><th width="240">Situation</th><th>Result</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>A player hit rung 20</td><td>They win — the game ended the instant they reached the summit.</td></tr><tr><td>Full time, nobody at 20</td><td>Highest on the ladder wins.</td></tr><tr><td>Exact tie at the top</td><td>Declared a tie.</td></tr></tbody></table>

If **you** won, you get the champion celebration — confetti, the dancing cat, and the fanfare, turned all the way up.

## The awards

The full-time screen hands out a couple of honors derived from the whole game's history, like **"Most savage fix"** — the single most devastating piece of sabotage of the match.

## The confessions

This is the moment the game has been building to. All game, **landed fixes were anonymous** — you could see the ladder move but never who was quietly climbing on sabotage. At full time, the mask comes off:

{% hint style="success" %}
**"The confessions"** lists every fix that landed all game — who fixed whom, finally named. The friend who's been suspiciously climbing gets exposed, the person who never suspected a thing finds out they were the mark, and the whole table relitigates the last hour.
{% endhint %}

Backfired fixes were already public the moment they happened; the confessions are specifically about the ones that **worked** — the quiet, successful sabotage nobody caught.

## Rematch

From full time you can head straight back out to **pick the next fixture** and run it again. Same friends, new match, fresh grudges.
