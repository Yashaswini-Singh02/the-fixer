# THE FIXER

> _Watch the match together. Bet the segments in secret. **Fix your friends** and climb the ladder._

<figure><img src=".gitbook/assets/hero-wordmark.png" alt="THE FIXER title screen"><figcaption><p>The landing screen — the "THE FIXER" wordmark over the live fixture list.</p></figcaption></figure>

## What is THE FIXER?

**THE FIXER** is a second-screen party game for **2–8 friends** watching the same football match. You don't play against the match — you play against _each other_, using the match as the dice.

The game chops the match into **15-minute segments**. In every segment you get a fresh handful of coins and three quick yes/no questions about what's about to happen on the pitch — _will there be a goal? two corners? a card?_ Everyone bets **in secret**. When the segment ends, the real match decides who was right, and winners **climb a shared 20-rung ladder**.

The twist that gives the game its name: at any moment you can spend 2 coins to secretly **fix a friend**. If they have a bad segment, your sabotage pays off and _nobody knows it was you_ — until full time, when every fix is confessed.

{% hint style="info" %}
The match data is **real**. Live games stream in as they happen; past games replay from the archive at up to 20× speed, so a two-hour match becomes a ~6-minute party round.
{% endhint %}

## The 60-second pitch

* **Everyone watches one match** — on a TV, a stream, anything. Phones are the game.
* **Six segments**, 15 minutes each (plus extra time if the match goes long).
* **Three markets a segment:** ⚽ Goal · 🚩 Corners (2+) · 🟨 Card. Yes or No.
* **Bets are sealed.** You see _that_ your friends bet, never _what_.
* **Win → you climb.** Odds-based payouts turn correct calls into ladder rungs.
* **Fix a friend** for 2 coins. Their bad segment becomes your good one — anonymously.
* **First to rung 20 wins.** No one gets there? Highest at full time takes it.
