# Segments & Markets

## Segments

The match is divided into **segments** — 15-minute blocks of the match clock.

* **Segments 1–6** cover regulation time (0–15′, 15–30′, … 75–90′).
* **Segments 7–8** cover extra time, and only appear if the match actually goes to extra time.

At the start of every segment, three things happen:

* Every player is topped up to **10 fresh coins** (plus any bonuses you earned — see [Coins & Bonuses](coins.md)).
* **Unspent coins from the last segment vanish.** Use them or lose them.
* Three markets open with freshly priced odds.

## The three markets

Every segment asks the same three yes/no questions about what will happen **during that segment** (not the whole match):

<table><thead><tr><th width="90">Market</th><th width="220">The question</th><th>Resolves YES when…</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>⚽ <strong>GOAL</strong></td><td>Will there be a goal this segment?</td><td><strong>1 or more goals</strong> are scored during the segment.</td></tr><tr><td>🚩 <strong>CORNERS</strong></td><td>Will there be 2+ corners this segment?</td><td><strong>2 or more corners</strong> are awarded during the segment.</td></tr><tr><td>🟨 <strong>CARD</strong></td><td>Will there be a card this segment?</td><td><strong>1 or more cards</strong> (yellow or red) are shown during the segment.</td></tr></tbody></table>

Each market is a simple **YES / NO** bet with its own live odds. You can bet on all three, one, or none.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/market-cards.png" alt="Three market cards: Goal, Corners, Card, each with YES/NO odds"><figcaption><p>A segment's three market cards, each showing the live YES and NO payouts.</p></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="info" %}
Outcomes are measured **from the segment's opening count**. If the match already had 4 corners when the segment opened, CORNERS still needs **2 more** during this segment to resolve YES. Each segment is its own clean slate.
{% endhint %}

## How outcomes are decided

Outcomes are read straight from the **real match feed** — goals, corners, and cards that actually happen on the pitch. There's no house, no randomness, and no judgement call: if the match shows a card in the 22nd minute, the CARD market for that segment is YES. Full stop.

## Special match events

Two things from the real match reach into the game beyond the markets:

* **Red card** — when a red card is shown, everyone **above rung 10** on the ladder slides **down 1 rung**. A twist of fate that punishes the leaders. See [The Ladder](the-ladder.md).
* **Half time / full time / extra time** — the game follows the match's real phases. Segments open and close on the actual whistle, not a fixed timer.
