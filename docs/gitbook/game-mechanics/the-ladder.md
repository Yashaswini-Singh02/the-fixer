# The Ladder

The ladder is the scoreboard. It's a shared **20-rung** climb, one lane per player, and it sits center-stage in the match room the whole game. Everything else — bets, odds, fixes — is just a way to move up it.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/ladder.png" alt="The 20-rung ladder with players climbing their lanes"><figcaption><p>The ladder: one lane per player, rungs bolder every five, glowing trails marking progress toward the summit.</p></figcaption></figure>

## Going up

You climb when your bets win and when your **fixes** land:

* **Winning bets** convert to rungs by stake × odds (see [Betting & Odds](betting-and-odds.md)). Every win is worth **at least 1** rung and **at most 6** per market.
* **A landed fix** climbs you **1–3 rungs**, scaled by how likely your target was to win (see [Fixing Your Friends](fixing.md)).

When you climb, your lane lights up: a pulse ring, a floating **"+N"**, and — if it's you — confetti, a dancing cat 🐈, and a chiptune fanfare.

## Coming down

There's only **one** way to lose ground, and it's not your fault:

{% hint style="danger" %}
**Red card.** When a red card is shown in the real match, **everyone above rung 10** slides **down 1 rung**. It only touches the leaders — a great equalizer late in a tense match.
{% endhint %}

A losing bet never pushes you down. A wrong call just costs you the coins you staked (which were going to vanish anyway).

## Hidden climbs

Here's the subtle part: when a **fix lands**, the rungs you steal are added to your climb — but they're **hidden from everyone else's view** of the ladder until full time. Your total looks like it came from your bets. Nobody can tell a suspiciously good segment from an honest one, which is exactly what keeps fixing anonymous. See [Fixing Your Friends](fixing.md) for the full secrecy model.

Reaching the top rung is marked **"First to 20 🏆"** at the summit. When it's won, head to **[Full Time](full-time.md)** for the podium, the awards, and the confessions.
